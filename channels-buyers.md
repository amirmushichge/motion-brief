# Channels & Buyers — Where to Sell Animated Brand Assets
*For Motion Brief by AmirMushich*

---

## Level 1 — Direct to Brand / Client

### Who buys
- DTC brands (Shopify-based, consumer products)
- Early-stage startups (pre-Series A, active on social)
- Small businesses running paid ads
- Founders building pitch decks

### Where to find them

| Platform | Use | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Contra | Portfolio + direct hire, no commission, US-skewed | ⭐⭐⭐ |
| Dribbble | Visual discovery, design-literate clients | ⭐⭐⭐ |
| Behance | Deep case studies, brand-led clients | ⭐⭐ |
| Upwork | Volume, broad demand, higher competition | ⭐⭐ |
| LinkedIn | Founders, CMOs, startup teams | ⭐⭐⭐ |

### Best first move
Strong portfolio on Dribbble or Contra +
sharp LinkedIn profile with motion samples.

## Level 2 — Brandbook Upsell

### Who buys
- Existing design clients (anyone you've built a brand for)
- Startup founders mid-rebrand
- Marketing leads at growing companies

### Pitch angle
"Your brandbook now includes deployment-ready motion assets
for email, ads, and pitch decks.
Same project. Same timeline. Stronger deliverable."

*No extra pitch needed. It's standard in your process.*

---

## Level 3 — Agency White-Label

### Who buys
Branding agencies (5–50 people) that:
- Have strong static brand work, weak or absent motion
- Use "flexible team" or contractor language on their site
- Offer web design, SaaS branding, product launches

### Where to find them
- Google: `"branding agency" + SaaS / fintech / AI / DTC`
- LinkedIn: search "Brand Studio" / "Creative Studio" / "White Label Design"
  Filter: Marketing & Advertising, 2–50 employees, US/EU
- Design subscription agencies (ManyPixels, Kimp, Design Pickle)
  → their clients and downstream agencies are also targets

### How to qualify a target
✓ Strong static brand portfolio + no motion anywhere
✓ Mentions "video" or "animation" in services but work looks mid
✓ Subscription or retainer model (constant output demand)

## Outreach — Cold Email Structure

**Line 1:** Specific compliment on their work
*"Loved your [Client] rebrand — static system is super tight."*

**Line 2:** Gap + opportunity
*"Noticed the launch assets were mostly static — a motion layer usually lifts perceived polish and ad performance for SaaS clients."*

**Line 3:** Your position
*"I'm a motion-driven brand director working white-label with studios on logo reveals, hero animations, and social loops you ship under your name."*

**Line 4:** Low-friction offer
*"I can send a one-page motion add-ons menu you can plug into proposals."*

**Line 5:** CTA
*"Worth a 15-minute call next week?"*

---

## Subject Lines — Tested Formats

**Ultra-short (high open rate)**
- "Motion support for your next launch"
- "White-label motion for your studio"
- "Adding motion to your rebrands"

**Personalized to their work**
- "Loved your [Client] rebrand – motion idea"
- "Turning your static identities into motion"

**Partnership framing**
- "[Your name] × [Agency] motion partnership"
- "More motion for your clients, no extra headcount"

**Capacity angle**
- "Reliable motion partner for busy weeks"
- "Senior motion, zero hiring overhead"

## Packaging Your Offer (Level 3)

Create a simple one-page PDF or Notion page:

**"Motion add-ons for branding projects"**
- Logo reveal
- Hero animation
- Social loops (multiple ratios)
- Product tour snippets
- Launch kit

Include:
→ Clear deliverables
→ Internal pricing (your rate)
→ Suggested client-facing price (2–3× your rate)
→ Process: brief → frames → animation → revisions → delivery

Two engagement modes:
- **Per-project:** scoped bundles for individual proposals
- **Retainer:** fixed days/month as their dedicated motion partner
